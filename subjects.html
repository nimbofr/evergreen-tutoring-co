Detailed curriculum & example learning sequences

Math — from number sense to confident problem-solving

K–1 Foundations

Outcomes: solid number sense, accurate counting, early addition/subtraction.

  • Number Sense: count to 100; compare numbers using symbols and words; use 10-frames & number lines.
  • Operations: add/sub within 10 (then 20) using manipulatives, drawings, and story problems.
  • Geometry & Measure: identify & describe 2D shapes; measure length/weight with nonstandard units.
  • Word Problems: pick the operation based on visuals; explain “how you know.”

Activities: “Grocery sort,” dice addition races, build-and-trace shapes.

Checks: quick fluency sprints, exit tickets, parent-friendly goal trackers.

Grades 2–3 Growth

Outcomes: fluent add/sub; strong base for multiplication/division.

  • Place Value: to 1,000; regrouping with base-10 blocks; mental strategies.
  • Facts & Fluency: add/sub within 100; arrays & equal groups (2s–10s) lay the foundation for multiplication.
  • Data & Time: read clocks to nearest 5; money word problems; bar/picture graphs.
  • Word Problems: one–two step, model-first strategies, explain your reasoning.

Activities: “Array city,” coin-count shop, graph the class survey.

Checks: balanced fact practice, strategy conferences, mini-quizzes.

Grades 4–5 Mastery

Outcomes: multi-step problem solving; fraction/decimal proficiency.

  • Operations: multi-digit add/sub/multiply/divide with place-value models and algorithms.
  • Fractions/Decimals: equivalence; compare/order; add/sub; tenths/hundredths in context.
  • Measurement & Geometry: area/volume/angles; coordinate grids; unit conversions.
  • Word Problems: multi-step with estimation & “reasonableness” checks.

Activities: “Recipe doubles” (fraction ops), angle scavenger hunt, budget projects.

Checks: mixed-problem journals, reflections, end-of-unit performance tasks.

Science — curiosity, investigation, and clear lab habits

K–1 Exploration

Outcomes: observe, ask questions, record with pictures/labels.

  • Living vs. non-living; weather & seasons; needs of plants/animals.
  • Simple investigations (magnets, ramps) with “predict → observe → share.”
  • Science talk stems (“I notice… I wonder… because…”).

Activities: seed journals, sink/float charting, weather calendars.

Checks: labeled drawings, oral explanations, vocabulary cards.

Grades 2–3 Foundations

Outcomes: testable questions & simple data tables.

  • Habitats & food chains; states of matter; light & sound basics.
  • Plan simple procedures; collect & organize data; read simple charts.
  • Read short science texts; identify claim and evidence.

Activities: shadow tracking, melting/evaporation mini-labs, habitat models.

Checks: data-table completion, CER (Claim–Evidence–Reasoning) statements.

Grades 4–5 Inquiry

Outcomes: fair tests, variable control, concise lab write-ups.

  • Earth systems, energy & motion; ecosystems & human impact.
  • Independent/dependent variables; trials & averages; graph interpretation.
  • Write procedures, analyze results, state limitations.

Activities: ramp racers, water cycle in a bag, energy transfer demos.

Checks: rubric-based lab reports, mini-presentations, vocabulary quizzes.

History & Social Studies — maps, culture, and civic sense

K–1 Community

Outcomes: recognize roles, rules, and simple timelines/maps.

  • Family & classroom rules; community helpers; classroom jobs.
  • Begin maps (home → school); cardinal directions basics; map keys.
  • Personal timelines with photos and captions.

Activities: “My community map,” helper interviews, 3-event timelines.

Checks: map keys, oral sharing, picture-based sort/sequence.

Grades 2–3 Connections

Outcomes: compare communities & cultures; use simple sources.

  • U.S. symbols & landmarks; local history snapshots.
  • Early civilizations overview; similarities/differences charts.
  • Primary vs. secondary sources; cause & effect in short texts.

Activities: artifact inference, postcard from a landmark, culture collage.

Checks: compare/contrast paragraphs, short source questions.

Grades 4–5 Inquiry

Outcomes: U.S. regions & government basics; research skills.

  • Physical & human geography; map skills & data reading.
  • Government branches basics; rights & responsibilities.
  • Inquiry projects with citations; present findings clearly.

Activities: “State expo,” government role-play, map labs.

Checks: research outlines, presentation rubrics, quizzes.

Reading & Writing — structured literacy to research writing

K–1 Literacy

Outcomes: decode CVC words, read simple texts, write sentences.

  • Phonemic awareness (blend, segment); phonics (CVC, digraphs).
  • High-frequency words; guided reading for fluency & confidence.
  • Sentence writing with capitals, spacing, end marks.

Activities: word building, picture-sentence journals, partner reads.

Checks: weekly decodable checks, sight-word trackers, dictation.

Grades 2–3 Reading to Learn

Outcomes: paragraph writing, main idea/details, growing stamina.

  • Fluency routines; retell & summarize with graphic organizers.
  • Main idea & supporting details; basic inference with text evidence.
  • Write clear paragraphs; intro grammar & revising.

Activities: “Paragraph puzzles,” RACES responses, book chats.

Checks: running records, short responses, revision checklists.

Grades 4–5 Author’s Craft

Outcomes: multi-paragraph essays, research & citations, editing.

  • Summarize multi-section texts; compare themes & structures.
  • Plan/draft/revise essays (informative/opinion/narrative).
  • Research notes, quoting/paraphrasing, bibliography basics.

Activities: CER journals, peer review, mini-research with slides.

Checks: writing rubrics, annotated drafts, conferencing notes.

What parents can expect

Short, consistent practice

We recommend 10–20 minute practice windows, 4–5 times per week. We provide printable routines and quick games so families can support learning without stress.

Clear feedback

After sessions, you’ll get a short note: skills covered, what went well, what’s next, and a simple at-home tip. We set 2–3 concrete targets per month and track progress together.
