Subjects & curriculum (K–5)

Aligned with common state standards and best practices in personalized learning. We combine direct instruction, guided practice, and joyful projects to build confidence and mastery.

Math

K–1

Counting to 100, comparing numbers, shapes, simple addition/subtraction with manipulatives, story problems with pictures.

2–3

Place value to 1,000, addition/subtraction fluency, arrays & intro multiplication, time & money, data & bar graphs.

4–5

Multi-digit operations, factors & multiples, fractions & decimals, measurement & geometry, multi‑step word problems.

Science

K–1

What living things need, weather & seasons, five senses, simple experiments (sink/float, magnets, ramps).

2–3

Habitats & food chains, solids/liquids/gases, light & sound, data collection with tables & simple charts.

4–5

Earth systems & cycles, energy & motion, fair‑test investigations, reading scientific texts & writing lab reports.

History & Social Studies

K–1

Community helpers, rules & responsibilities, maps & globes, family culture & traditions.

2–3

Local & national symbols, early civilizations, comparing communities, timelines & sequence.

4–5

U.S. geography, state regions, government basics, research skills & primary sources.

Reading & Writing

K–1

Phonological awareness, phonics & decoding, high‑frequency words, shared reading, handwriting.

2–3

Fluency, main idea & details, paragraph writing, grammar basics, reading response journals.

4–5

Summarizing & inference, multi‑paragraph essays, research projects, revision & editing.

Formats

  • 1:1 Tutoring: fully personalized pacing.
  • Small Groups (2–4 students): peers at similar levels; collaborative practice.
  • Homework Help & Study Skills: planners, checklists, and “show your thinking” routines.
