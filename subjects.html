Deep dives & sample activities

Math — build fluency and problem-solving games & manipulatives

K–1

  • Counting to 100, ten-frames, number bonds
  • Shapes & measurement with real objects
  • Activity: “Grocery sort” — group items by attribute, add/sub within 10

2–3

  • Place value to 1,000, regrouping fluency
  • Arrays → multiplication facts; time & money
  • Activity: “Array city” — build cityscapes from equal groups

4–5

  • Multi-digit operations, factors & multiples
  • Fractions/decimals; geometry & measurement
  • Activity: “Recipe doubles” — fraction ops with real recipes
Science — curiosity through fair tests hands-on labs

K–1

  • Living/non-living, weather & seasons
  • Observe & record with pictures
  • Activity: “Sink or float” prediction charts

2–3

  • Habitats, food chains; matter & states
  • Data tables & simple graphs
  • Activity: “Shadow chase” — light & measurement

4–5

  • Earth systems, energy & motion
  • Plan & run fair tests; write lab reports
  • Activity: “Ramp racers” — variables & averages
History & Social Studies — maps, culture, and civic basics primary sources

K–1

  • Community helpers, rules & routines
  • Maps & timelines with photos
  • Activity: “My timeline” — 3-event personal history

2–3

  • Local & national symbols, early cultures
  • Compare communities; cause & effect
  • Activity: “Artifact mystery” — observe → infer

4–5

  • U.S. regions & gov’t basics; inquiry projects
  • Read/annotate primary & secondary sources
  • Activity: “State expo” — research & present
Reading & Writing — from phonics to research structured literacy

K–1

  • Phonemic awareness, phonics & decoding
  • High-frequency words; read-alouds
  • Activity: “Word builders” with tiles

2–3

  • Fluency; main idea & details; paragraphing
  • Grammar & sentence combining
  • Activity: “Paragraph puzzles” (topic → details)

4–5

  • Summaries & inference; multi-paragraph essays
  • Research, quoting, and revision cycles
  • Activity: “Claim–evidence–reasoning” journals

Study tips that work at home

Short, frequent practice

10–15 minute bursts, 4–5x/week, beat long cram sessions. Use timers and celebrate small wins.

Show your thinking

Have students talk through their steps or annotate. We model sentence starters and math diagrams.
